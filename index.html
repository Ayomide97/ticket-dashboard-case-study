01 Context

As part of my role with a global technology client (under NDA), I was responsible for creating an internal dashboard to improve visibility and efficiency in managing support tickets. Due to NDA restrictions, visuals and client details are omitted — this case study focuses on my process and impact.

Global Team

Distributed support teams across multiple time zones

NDA Protected

Confidential project with restricted visual disclosure

Internal Tool

Dashboard designed for internal team efficiency

02 Problem

Support teams faced several challenges that impacted their efficiency and ability to provide timely assistance.

Limited Visibility

Limited visibility across open and resolved tickets

Poor Prioritization

Difficulty prioritising urgent issues

Fragmented Workflows

Fragmented workflows spread across multiple systems

03 My Role

Dashboard Creation

Built the dashboard directly using the client's internal design and development toolset (not Figma)

UX/UI Design Input

Structured layouts for KPI cards, tables, and charts to maximise clarity

Collaboration

Worked closely with managers and support agents to ensure workflows matched real needs

04 Process

Visuals shown are abstract wireframes; not actual client screens

1

Research & Requirements

  • Partnered with stakeholders to identify ticketing pain points
  • Mapped critical user journeys: ticket creation, prioritisation, escalation, and closure

Ticket lifecycle mapping showing key stages and potential friction points

2

Dashboard Build

  • Designed the information hierarchy directly in the internal tool
  • Configured KPI counters (Closed, Open, High Priority, SLA Breaches, SEVs) for quick insights
  • Integrated filters (year, month, day) and data tables for drill-downs

Wireframe showing dashboard structure with key metrics and filtering capabilities

3

Testing & Feedback

  • Shared working versions with support leads
  • Iterated based on feedback around clarity, prioritisation, and SLA monitoring
